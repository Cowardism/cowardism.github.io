"Cowardists" and "Cowardism"
   - Newest Words in our Vocabulary


It has become apparent that the word "terrorism" does not accurately reflect the true nature of that horrendous crime. Instead, it seems to glorify the crime due to the symbolism associated with that word – whenever the media reports on such horrendous acts as “terrorism”, the terrorist groups seem to take great pride in hearing it. In fact, that word itself serves as propaganda for the terrorist organisations.

What is the true nature of the crime which we currently refer to as "terrorism"? Is it not the cowardly act of killing of innocent and unarmed civilians – including children? Let's take a closer look at the meaning behind the word "cowardly". The English Oxford dictionary defines "cowardly" as:

cowardly:
adjective. 1. Lacking courage. 2 (of an action) carried out against a person who is unable to retaliate. "a cowardly attack on a helpless victim"

Based on the second definition above, we propose the introduction of 2 new words into our vocabulary we believe would better represent the crime and the criminal: “cowardism” and “cowardist”:

cowardist:
noun. A person that carries out a cowardly attack on unarmed civilians as means of coercion.
cowardism:
noun. Violent action carried out through cowardly attacks on unarmed civilians as means of coercion.

The new words proposed above are derived using the second definition of "cowardly". But does the first definition, "Lacking courage" also apply to cowardists? After all, aren’t they courageous enough to take their own lives for whatever cause they believe it? The fact is, we will never fully understand the corrupted state of mind of a cowardist. Besides any brainwashing they may have undergone, cowardists are also known to use drugs such as Captagon to induce “chemical courage” right before carrying out an attack (see "References" section below for more information). Regardless of the perceived “courage” or their disguised cowardice, the terms “cowardists” and “cowardism” primarily refers to the carrying out of cowardly attacks on unarmed civilians.


Most cowardists are known to be depressed individuals that have suffered some injustice in the past or have failed in their pursuits in life. They are easily recruited by cowardist organisations (eg ISIS, Al-Qaeda, Boko-Haram...etc) and they begin to realise being a “terrorist” is an easy path to notoriety. They allow themselves to believe that this killing of unarmed civilians can be justified. No matter how they have been brainwashed, the killing of innocent civilians without giving them any opportunity to defend themselves is simply a cowardly act – cowardism.

Let's Change our Vocabulary

We are advocating a change in our vocabulary whenever we refer to “terrorism” or “terrorists” – we believe “cowardism” or “cowardists” are more appropriate as:

  • Our language is full of symbolism – words such as “terrorism” has the contradictory connotation of glorifying the crime of attacking soft targets (eg unarmed civilians in a mall, church or airport). The use of such words serves as propaganda for the terrorist organisations and inspires new recruits.
  • The word “cowardism” on the other hand, recognises those attacks on innocent civilians without glorifying the crime.
  • Once terms such as “cowardism”, “cowardist attack” or “cowardist bomber” start to become commonly used by the public at a global scale – the terrorist organisations will find it harder and harder to find inspiration for these attacks – even if they continue to refer to these by other names such as “Jihad” or “martyrdom”. The symbolism of the language used by the public will always have overarching influence.

Global Campaign 2019

Given words such as “terrorism” and “terrorists” are so widely used, is it possible for us to bring about a change in our vocabulary to refer to terrorism using a word that is more befitting of the crime - “cowardism”? To do this, we will need a change of perspective at a global scale! We believe this can be done with your assistance!

Here is our ambitious goal which we hope to achieve in 2019 - "Global Campaign 2019":

By 21st of July 2019 (which is the 3-month anniversary of the cowardism attack on the beautiful island of Sri Lanka), we'd like to see journalist and media organisations around the globe starting to refer to “terrorist attacks” as "cowardist attacks" and “terrorism” as "cowardism".


The media will only use these new words if they have become mainstream in our written/spoken language! This is where we need your help: Please spread our message through social media and begin using these new words in all your personal interactions - so these new words can start to enter into the mainstream! We need to make this message to go viral now if we can ever hope for the media to pick it up in 3 months time!!!

Here are some of the examples of vocabulary changes we’re hoping to see reported in the media and enter into our common spoken language:

  • “terrorist” to be only referred to as “cowardist”
  • “suicide bomber” to be only referred to as “cowardist bomber”
  • “terrorism” to be only referred to as “cowardism”
  • “terrorist organisations” to be only referred to as “cowardist organisations”

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I introduce the new word "cowardists" to all my friends, family and colleagues?

The easiest way is to first send them the link to the website or share it through Facebook. Then next time you meet them, you can openly discuss it and start using the correct vocabulary as a group

Changing our vocabulary around terrorism is a unique way of tackling the problem. Has this been done before?

Not that we know of - But there are similarities. For example, school and workplace bullying can be tackled just by asking "Are you being a bully?". The correct labelling and recognition of that unacceptable behaviour is usually enough to deter the bully. Similarly, the recognition of cowardly killing of unarmed civilians through use of the word "cowardism" will make that crime less inspiring for future terrorists. However, this needs to be a global movement and involves the media also applying the correct vocabulary when reporting such crime.

From time to time I still use the word "terrorist" as I forget to use the word "cowardist". What can I do to improve?

Yes, when we first came up with the new words, it was difficult for us to use them initially too. This is because the words "terrorist" and "terrorism" are so deeply ingrained in our minds after constant exposure through the media. It will get a lot easier soon once the media also start using the correct vocabulary. In the meantime, try saying out loud the word "cowardism" three or four times. This will help to instil it in our minds.

All my friends, family and colleagues have already started to use the right vocabulary. But I want to do more to help. Any suggestions?

It's great that you are taking the initiative in driving this further. We need this to be a global movement - please reach out to your overseas friends too. Through social media and other means, pls ask them to share it with their friends. Please also write to local media broadcasters (eg radio, TV, newspapers...etc) and ask them to discuss whether it is possible to run a media segment to introduce the public to the new words and to openly discuss it.

Where can I get more information? Is there a forum where we can further discuss the new vocabulary?

Setting up an online forum is something we are planning on doing...However, not in the short term. At the moment we are encouraging everyone to discuss the new vocabulary through social media.

How will you be driving this Global Campaign 2019?

As we are a non-profit organisation, we are unable to allocate any funds for any media advertising. However, we will be advocating the new vocabulary through social media and by writing to various media organisations around the world. We seek your assistance in doing the same within your network of friends, relatives and colleagues. It needs to be a global scale solution and we all need to contribute if we are to succeed.
