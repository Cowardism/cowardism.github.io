    "Cowardists" and "Cowardism" - Newest Words in our Vocabulary
     

    It is clear the word "terrorist" does not reflect the true nature of horrendous crime that is terrorism. Instead, it seems to glorify the crime – whenever the media reports such horrendous acts as “terrorism”, the terrorist groups seem to take great pride in hearing this. In fact, that word itself is propaganda for the terrorist groups.

    Let me repeat: The word “terrorist” does not reflect the true nature of their crime. The true nature of their crime is the cowardly act of killing of innocent and unarmed civilians – including children. Hence a better term would be “cowardist”:

         cowardist: noun. Person that kill or injure unarmed civilians as means of coercion.
         cowardism: noun. Act of killing or hurting unarmed civilians as means of coercion.

    It is imperative that we no longer glorify this atrocious crime by calling it “terrorism”. Referring to it as “cowardism” will require a change in mindset – but it is achievable and will be the first step in winning the so-called “war against terror”.

    Some may question whether cowardists are really cowards – after all, aren’t they brave enough to sacrifice their lives for their cause? It is important to note that a brainwashed death-wish does not really equate to “bravery”. These cowardists are very mediocre and depressed individuals that have failed in all their pursuits in life. They see “terrorism” as the easiest way for them to get notoriety. They then allow themselves to believe that this cowardly killing of unarmed civilians is in fact justified. No matter how they have been brainwashed, killing innocent people without giving them any opportunity to defend themselves is simply a cowardly act - cowardism. 

    There are similarities between bullies and cowardists. It is well recognised that most bullies take pleasure in hurting those who are at a disadvantage. They are regarded as cowards on the inside – and they usually retreat when the victim or a bystander decides to make a stand. One could argue that a cowardist is an even bigger coward than the typical school yard bully – as they do not even give an opportunity for the victims or bystanders to intervene.

    The Global Challenge
     

    Is it possible for us to bring about a change in our vocabulary to refer to terrorism using a word that is more befitting of the crime - “cowardism”? To do this, we will need a change of perspective at a global scale!

    Let’s be clear as to why it is important for us all to campaign for this change:

    • The word “terrorism” or “terrorist” glorifies the crime committed. Terrorist organisations that commit the crime will always proudly accept responsibility after such a crime is committed. After the recent Sri Lanka bombings, it was reported that “ISIS Claims Responsibility for Sri Lanka Terrorist Attack”. However, if the newspapers had reported this act as “ISIS Claims Responsibility for Sri Lanka Cowardist Attack”, we are sure that ISIS would not be using that newspaper as part of their future recruitment drives.
    • If “terrorist organisations” are globally referred to as a “cowardist organisation”, it will give second thoughts to those who would consider funding such organisations.
    • The semantics of such words can be very powerful – The same crime can be reported as both “terrorism” and “cowardism” – but one glorifies the act (and serve as propaganda for the terrorist organisations) while the other is more demeaning to the perpetrators.
    • “Terrorism” propaganda seems to promote suicide bombers as being brave and their crimes justifiable. However, this is far from the truth. Instead, they operate from a deluded, depressed, and a brainwashed state of mind. Such a corrupted and fragmented mind is simply incapable of understanding bravery in any sense of that word. It is time we call it out for what it really is – it is a cowardice act inflicted on easy targets that often include children. The word “cowardism” better conveys the cowardly act of taking the lives of unarmed civilians.

    So, is it possible for us to change our vocabulary to refer to such acts as “cowardism”? Given words such as “terrorism” is so deeply ingrained in our minds, it will be a monumental challenge. However, given the widespread use of social media, this might not be as difficult as it would have been 10 years ago.

    Our ambitious Global Challenge: By 21st of July 2019 (which is the 3-month anniversary of the cowardism attack on the beautiful island of Sri Lanka), we'd like to see all journalist and media outlets around the globe to refer to any terrorist attack as "cowardist attack" and terrorism as "cowardism".

    Before the above can be achieved, we need to start this vocabulary change now through all of our interactions on social media and personal conversations. We need your assistance with this – and at a global scale! Only if we start this now can we ever hope journalists and news reporters will be in a position to change their vocabulary as well.

    If you chose to accept this global challenge: we’d like to see the following changes to our vocabulary in all social media and personal interactions/conversations:
    • “terrorist” to be only referred to as “cowardist”
    • “suicide bomber” to be only referred to as “cowardist bomber”
    • “terrorism” to be only referred to as “cowardism”
    • “terrorist organisations” to be only referred to as “cowardist organisations”
